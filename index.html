Rule of Nines (Adult TBSA%)

The Rule of Nines is a standardized method to quickly assess the percentage of total body surface area (TBSA) affected by a burn in adults. Hover over the chart sections to see the corresponding body part and percentage.

First Aid Guidelines

Immediate first aid for burns focuses on cooling, pain relief, and infection prevention. Follow these detailed steps to provide effective initial care. Always seek medical attention for severe burns or if unsure about the severity.

Detailed Steps

    Important Considerations

      Criteria for Burn Center Admission

      Specialized care is crucial for complex or severe burns. This checklist outlines the American Burn Association's criteria for determining when a patient should be transferred to a designated burn center. These guidelines help ensure patients receive the appropriate level of care to manage complications and optimize recovery.

        Clinical Management After Burn Injury

        Effective management following a burn injury is a multidisciplinary effort, encompassing immediate medical stabilization, surgical intervention, and comprehensive physical therapy. This section details the key strategies in each domain, from initial resuscitation to advanced rehabilitative techniques aimed at restoring function and minimizing long-term complications.
